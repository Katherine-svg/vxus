Understanding premium/discount

Premium and discount information is calculated to illustrate the historical differences between an ETF's market price and the value of its underlying securities, or its NAV. Because ETFs are traded throughout the day on a stock exchange, their prices may at times vary from the value of their underlying securitiesâ€”trading at a premium with significant buying demand and at a discount with selling demand. This disconnect is typically corrected through an ETF's unique creation and redemption process. In the case of a premium, an Authorized Participant would create shares of an ETF by buying the underlying securities, delivering them to the fund provider in exchange for ETF shares, which would, in turn, be sold until the premium has eroded. As a result, price differences tend to be fleeting.
